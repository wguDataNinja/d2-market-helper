Diablo 2 Resurrected Items – Loot, Item Types, and Trading Options

When you think of Diablo 2 Resurrected (or D2R), the first thing that likely comes to mind is the loot — endless amounts of loot and the thrill of chasing it. Diablo 2's itemization system is iconic, known for its clever balance of RNG and reward pacing. The game is generous with lower-rarity items but can be notoriously stingy when it comes to Uniques and Sets. Sometimes you get lucky with a Unique drop in the first act — other times, you might go hours without finding anything meaningful. That’s when many players turn to trading and decide to buy Diablo 2 Resurrected items from other players or marketplaces like Odealo.

Diablo 2’s loot system is often described as “RNG done right.” It constantly tempts you with decent upgrades and, just when you’re about to give up, throws a rare item your way to keep the dopamine flowing. With so many powerful and valuable items in the game, it's no surprise that people still sell Diablo 2 items over two decades after the original release. Below, we’ll break down D2R’s item types and rarities and show you the best way to gear up — whether by farming or deciding to buy D2R items directly.

Item Types in Diablo 2 Resurrected

Diablo 2 Resurrected offers a wide variety of item types that keep players engaged for hundreds of hours. Here’s a breakdown of the major item categories — many of which you can loot, craft, or buy Diablo 2 Resurrected items online:

  • Ammunition – Arrows and Bolts used for Bows and Crossbows. These can be bought from vendors and have no unique stats.
  • Potions – Include healing, mana, and stamina potions as well as throwable ones like Exploding or Choking Gas Potions. More powerful versions, like Full Rejuvenation Potions, can be crafted in the Horadric Cube.
  • Gems – Socketable items that provide bonuses to gear. There are five levels of quality (Chipped to Perfect), and three gems of the same type/quality can be combined into a higher-tier version.
  • Runes – Used to boost gear and to create powerful Runewords when placed in the right socketed items. Runes can be farmed or upgraded via the Cube. Many players choose to buy D2R items like high-tier runes from trusted sellers.
  • Jewels – Like Gems and Runes, they can be socketed but appear in magical, rare, or even unique variants. Also used in crafting.
  • Charms – Provide passive bonuses just by being in your inventory. They vary in size (1–3 slots) and are a key part of min-maxing.
  • Scrolls – Identify unknown items or open Town Portals. Can be stored in Tomes to save inventory space.
  • Keys – Used to unlock chests that often contain better loot. Always useful to carry a few.
  • Quest Items – Specific to quests and not tradable. Required to progress through the game's storyline.
  • Gear – This includes Weapons, Armor, Shields, and Jewelry. The most important item category and the one most commonly traded. Many players buy D2R gear to skip farming or finish their builds faster.

Item Qualities and Rarities

D2R items are divided into seven quality tiers, each with different stats, uses, and trading value:

  • Low Quality – Cracked, Crude, Damaged versions of gear. Weak stats (~75% of normal). Only useful at the very beginning. Usually sold to vendors.
  • Normal Quality – Standard gear. Sometimes found with sockets, which makes it valuable for Runewords. Often sold by NPCs.
  • Superior Quality – Better versions of normal items with bonus durability, damage, or defense. Occasionally have sockets, making them ideal bases for Runewords.
  • Magic Quality – Blue items with one or two random magical properties (prefixes/suffixes). Found frequently and enhanced by Magic Find gear.
  • Rare Quality – Yellow items with multiple properties. Much stronger than Magic items and often usable in mid- to late-game builds. One of the first powerful upgrades players receive.
  • Set Items – Green items with set bonuses that activate when multiple pieces are equipped. Full sets offer powerful effects, though collecting them can be tough without trading. Many players choose to buy Diablo 2 Resurrected set items instead of waiting for drops.
  • Unique Items – The rarest and most powerful items in the game. Each Unique has fixed (but slightly variable) properties and is often build-defining. Drop rates are extremely low, making them highly sought after. If you can’t find them naturally, the easiest solution is to buy Diablo 2 Unique items from a marketplace like Odealo.
